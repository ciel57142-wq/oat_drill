Stage 1 — Pure Observation
Notice one specific, neutral detail about the scene. Not "she's pretty" — the way she's holding her drink, the earring she keeps touching, the expression when her friend talks. Just observe. No assumption yet.

Stage 2 — Observation → Assumption
Take the observation and make the creative leap. Ask yourself: "What kind of person does this behavior suggest?" — then exaggerate it by 30%. Generate 3 versions: realistic, slightly absurd, fully ridiculous. The one you'd use is usually the middle one.

Stage 3 — Full OAT + Delivery
All three steps. Keep the tease short — one sentence max. Soft nickname or mock-warning. The assumption does the work. The tease just lands it. Say it out loud while writing.
